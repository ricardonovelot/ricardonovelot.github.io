Context

I've been a User Experience Designer at Whirlpool since November 2024. I work on connected appliances across the company's consumer mobile apps.

What the work is actually like

A connected appliance is a state machine that lives in someone's house. It can be idle, running, paused, done, or suddenly unreachable. My job is to map those states and design the multi step flows that carry people through them on a phone. That includes the edge cases where the device and the app stop agreeing about what is happening.

I work through what each state means, and what the app should do when a flow breaks partway through, with product managers and engineers.

Design standards work

I established the team's design standards, workflows, and best practices. I set up the team's Jira workflow, and it worked well enough that the rest of the UX org adopted it. A lot of that work also happened in Figma: our ways of working, handoff flows, and specs.

I also built Demos, an internal tool to test high fidelity concepts on real devices. It has its own case study.
