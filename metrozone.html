MetroZone: redesigning T-Mobile's Android content app

I worked on the redesign of MetroZone and maintained the product's design system of several hundred components. In user testing, satisfaction with the redesign rose 30%.

Context

I was a Product Designer at Digital Turbine from September 2021 to August 2023. The product I want to show from that time is MetroZone, a carrier exclusive content platform on Metro by T-Mobile Android phones: news, games, and daily challenges, built to be a daily stop for engaging content.

The task

Modernize a well established app that was showing signs of aging in its visual design. Working with the product team, the redesign focused on five fronts: accessibility, hierarchy, iconography, brand consistency, and typography.

The design system work

The product ran on a design system of several hundred UI components. Maintaining and updating it was my responsibility. That sounds like housekeeping, and some days it was. But a redesign puts a component library under real pressure. Patterns move faster than the library does, and if the two drift apart people stop trusting the system. A steady part of my job was keeping them in sync, and by the end of the redesign the entire component library had been updated.

Outcome

User satisfaction rose 30% in user testing of the redesign. The full before and after is below, and in the MetroZONE project on Behance.

Where that work led: Moment

When a client came to Digital Turbine with a vision for a news app, we didn't start from zero. Moment is what we built: a news reader where the stories, the weather, and a podcast player live in one feed.

This time the reading happened inside the app, with an in app reader, a podcast player, dark and light modes, and a share sheet.
